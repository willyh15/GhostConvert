PNG to JPG Guide

A quick guide to converting PNG images into lightweight JPGs for web, thumbnails, and previews.

PNG is a great format for crisp graphics and transparency, but it often produces larger files. JPG is designed for smaller file sizes and fast loading, especially for photos and thumbnail style images.

If you want to convert right now, use the tool here: PNG to JPG Converter.

When PNG to JPG is the right move

  • Photos that do not need transparency
  • Thumbnails and previews for web pages
  • Product images for listings and ecommerce
  • Images you plan to email or upload to a form with size limits

When you should keep PNG

  • Logos and icons with transparency
  • UI screenshots with sharp text
  • Graphics with hard edges and flat colors

What happens to transparency

JPG does not support transparency. Any transparent areas in your PNG will become a solid background, usually white. If your PNG is a logo with transparent edges, you may see a halo effect if the logo was designed to blend into a dark background.

If you need a specific background color, place the PNG on that color in an editor first, then convert.

Tips for clean results

  • Use the original PNG file rather than one re-saved by a chat app
  • For photos, JPG is usually the best balance of size and clarity
  • For logos, consider keeping PNG or using SVG when possible
  • If you also need smaller PNGs, use Compress Images

Frequently asked questions

Does converting to JPG make the image blurry?

JPG compression can soften sharp edges a bit, especially around text and line art. For photos and thumbnails it usually looks great. For logos and UI graphics, PNG is often the better choice.

Can I convert a transparent PNG and keep transparency?

Not with JPG. If you need transparency, keep PNG or use a format like WEBP or SVG depending on your use case.
