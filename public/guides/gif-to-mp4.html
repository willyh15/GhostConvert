How to Convert GIF to MP4

MP4 is usually much smaller than GIF and plays smoothly across modern apps, websites, and editors.

Quick steps

  1. Open the tool: GIF to MP4
  2. Upload your GIF file
  3. Select Convert to MP4
  4. Download the MP4
  5. Share it or drop it into your editor

Why MP4 is better than GIF in many cases

GIF is easy to post, but it is an older format that tends to create large files. MP4 uses modern video compression that stores the same motion more efficiently, often with smoother playback.

  • Smaller file sizes for faster uploads and downloads
  • Better playback performance on phones and laptops
  • Better compatibility with social apps and editing tools

What to expect after conversion

The MP4 keeps your frame order and timing. Most platforms will treat it like normal video, which is usually what you want.

  • Looping depends on the platform or player, not the MP4 file itself
  • If your GIF had transparency, the MP4 will use a solid background because standard MP4 does not support transparency

Tips for clean results

  • Start with the best quality GIF you have
  • Short clips compress especially well
  • If your GIF is blurry, the MP4 will match that blur, so begin from a sharper source when possible

Common questions

Will the MP4 loop automatically

MP4 does not loop by itself. Many players and social apps loop short videos automatically, and editors let you repeat clips easily.

Can I keep transparency

Standard MP4 does not support transparency. If you need transparency for overlays, consider keeping the original GIF or using an image sequence workflow.

How are my files handled

Files are stored only long enough to perform the conversion and provide a download link, then removed after a short period. See the Privacy Policy for details.
