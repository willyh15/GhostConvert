MP4 to MP3 Guide

Extract audio from MP4 videos and save it as MP3 for music, lectures, podcasts, meetings, and offline listening.

Why convert MP4 to MP3?

A lot of useful audio lives inside video files. Converting MP4 to MP3 keeps only the audio track, which usually means a much smaller file that’s easier to save, share, and play on any device.

How to convert MP4 to MP3 with GhostConvert

  1. Open the tool: MP4 to MP3.
  2. Drop your MP4 into the upload box (or click to browse).
  3. Click Convert to MP3.
  4. Download the MP3 and play it anywhere.

Common use cases

  • Saving lectures, sermons, classes, and meetings for offline listening.
  • Extracting music or audio from performance videos.
  • Turning screen recordings into audio only notes.
  • Creating audio clips for sharing or archiving.

Tips for best sound quality

  • Use the original MP4: avoid versions re-compressed by messaging apps.
  • If speech is the goal: pick the cleanest recording you have (less background noise).
  • Keep your MP4 backup: especially if you might edit or export later.

FAQ

Will the MP3 be the same length?

Yes: the duration stays the same. You’re just removing the video track and saving the audio as MP3.

Does MP4 → MP3 lower audio quality?

MP3 is compressed, so there can be some loss depending on the source. For most everyday listening, the output sounds clean while keeping file sizes smaller.

How long are files stored?

Files are kept only long enough to complete the conversion and briefly provide a download link, then deleted automatically as described in the Privacy Policy.
